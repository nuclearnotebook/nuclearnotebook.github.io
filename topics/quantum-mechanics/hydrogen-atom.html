The Only Exactly Solvable Atom

The hydrogen atom — one proton bound to one electron by the Coulomb force — is the only atom for which the Schrödinger equation has an exact analytic solution. Every other atom requires approximation methods (perturbation theory, variational methods, Hartree-Fock). The exact hydrogen solution provides the starting point for all of atomic physics and the framework for understanding the periodic table through the aufbau principle and shell structure.

Applying the Schrödinger equation with V(r) = −e²/(4πε₀r) in spherical coordinates and separating variables ψ(r,θ,φ) = R_nl(r)·Y_l^m(θ,φ) yields two differential equations: the angular equation (giving spherical harmonics Y_l^m) and the radial equation (giving the associated Laguerre polynomial solutions R_nl). The energy eigenvalues depend only on n:

Hydrogen Energy Levels (exact)E_n = −μe⁴/(8ε₀²h²) · 1/n² = −13.6056 eV/n²     n = 1, 2, 3, ...

Atomic Orbitals — Shapes of the Wave Functions

The orbital angular momentum quantum number l determines the shape of the probability distribution |ψ_nlm|²:

  • s orbitals (l=0): Spherically symmetric. The 1s orbital (ground state) has ψ_1s = (1/√π a₀³/²) e^(−r/a₀). Maximum probability at r = a₀ (Bohr radius). Higher s orbitals (2s, 3s) have radial nodes.
  • p orbitals (l=1): Dumbbell-shaped with nodal plane through origin. Three orientations: p_x, p_y, p_z (real combinations of m_l = 0, ±1 states). Two lobes of high probability along the orbital axis.
  • d orbitals (l=2): More complex shapes — cloverleaf for m_l = ±2, and combinations for m_l = 0, ±1. Essential for transition metal chemistry and colour.
  • f orbitals (l=3): Seven orientations. Responsible for lanthanide and actinide chemistry.

The Bohr Radius — The Scale of Atomic Physics

Bohr Radiusa₀ = 4πε₀ℏ²/(mₑe²) = ℏ/(mₑαc) = 0.52918 Å = 52.918 pm

The Bohr radius a₀ is the most probable electron-proton distance in the ground state (1s orbital) and sets the fundamental scale of atomic physics. All orbital sizes scale as n²a₀/Z for a hydrogenic ion of nuclear charge Z. For hydrogen-like ions: He⁺ (Z=2) has orbitals 2× smaller; Li²⁺ (Z=3) has orbitals 3× smaller.

Fine Structure and Beyond

The "exact" solution above ignores several corrections that become important for precision:

  • Relativistic correction: Electron velocity ~αc in ground state gives ΔE_rel ~ −α²E_n/4. Splits energy levels by n and l.
  • Spin-orbit coupling: Electron's spin magnetic moment interacts with magnetic field seen in electron's rest frame. Splits levels with l > 0 into doublets (j = l ± ½). Size: ΔE_SO ~ α²E_n.
  • Lamb shift: Quantum electrodynamic correction from electron interaction with vacuum fluctuations. Splits 2s₁/₂ and 2p₁/₂ (which are degenerate at the previous level) by 1057.8 MHz.
  • Hyperfine structure: Interaction of electron and proton magnetic moments. Splits 1s ground state by 1420.4 MHz — the famous 21-cm radio wavelength.
📡 The 21-cm Hydrogen Line

The hydrogen ground state (1s) is split by the hyperfine interaction (electron spin-proton spin coupling) into two states separated by ΔE = 5.87 μeV, corresponding to radiation at 1420.4 MHz (λ = 21.1 cm). Despite a natural transition lifetime of ~10⁷ years, the cosmic abundance of hydrogen (10⁵⁷ atoms in a typical galaxy) makes 21-cm the brightest radio line in the sky. It maps the Milky Way's spiral arms (photons penetrate dust that blocks visible light), measures galactic rotation curves (revealing dark matter), and traces the large-scale structure of hydrogen in the universe from z=0 to the epoch of reionisation at z~10. The Square Kilometre Array will use 21-cm intensity mapping to probe dark energy across cosmic time.
