Why We Need Quantum Fields

Non-relativistic quantum mechanics describes a fixed number of particles: the N-particle Schrödinger equation. But processes like pair creation (γ → e⁺e⁻), particle decay (π⁰ → 2γ), and scattering (e⁻e⁻ → e⁻e⁻ via photon exchange) involve changing particle numbers. These require a framework where particle creation and annihilation are fundamental operations — quantum field theory. QFT promotes classical fields (like the electromagnetic field A_μ(x)) to quantum operators, whose excitations are the particles we observe.

Field Quantisation — From Classical to Quantum

where ωₖ = √(k² + m²)c. The creation operator â†ₖ creates a particle of momentum ℏk and energy ℏωₖ; âₖ destroys one. The vacuum state |0⟩ has no particles but non-zero zero-point energy E₀ = Σₖ ℏωₖ/2 — an infinite sum regulated by renormalisation.

QED — Feynman Rules

Quantum electrodynamics describes the interaction between electrons (Dirac field ψ), positrons (ψ̄), and photons (gauge field A_μ). The QED Lagrangian:

QED Lagrangian Densityℒ_QED = ψ̄(iγ^μD_μ − mc/ℏ)ψ − ¼F_{μν}F^{μν}     D_μ = ∂_μ + ieA_μ/ℏc

The Feynman rules translate each diagram element into a mathematical factor: electron propagator S_F(p) = i(γ·p + m)/(p²−m²+iε), photon propagator D_F^{μν}(k) = −ig^{μν}/(k²+iε), and QED vertex −ieγ^μ/ℏ. The fine structure constant α = e²/(4πε₀ℏc) ≈ 1/137.036 is the expansion parameter — each additional photon line adds a factor ~α.

The Most Precise Test in Physics

The electron anomalous magnetic moment a_e = (g_e−2)/2 has been calculated in QED to 5th order in α (requiring evaluation of 10,000+ Feynman diagrams) and including small hadronic and electroweak corrections:

QED Prediction (5th order)a_e(theory) = 0.001 159 652 181 606 (229)
a_e(experiment) = 0.001 159 652 180 591 (026) (Harvard, 2022)

Agreement to 12 significant figures — the most precisely tested prediction in the history of science. The tiny ~2σ discrepancy may indicate physics beyond the Standard Model and is actively investigated.

Virtual Particles and the Casimir Effect

In Feynman diagrams, internal lines represent virtual particles — intermediate quantum states not satisfying E² = (pc)² + (mc²)². Their "existence" for time Δt ~ ℏ/(2ΔE) is consistent with the energy-time uncertainty principle. The Casimir effect provides macroscopic evidence for quantum vacuum fluctuations: two uncharged parallel conducting plates separated by d attract each other with pressure:

Casimir PressureP = −ℏcπ²/(240d⁴) ≈ −1.3×10⁻³ Pa at d = 1 μm

First measured by Sparnaay (1958), precisely confirmed by Lamoreaux (1997). The Casimir force is now measured routinely at sub-micron separations and must be accounted for in MEMS device design — a macroscopic, measurable consequence of the quantum field vacuum.

🌠 Renormalisation

Naive QFT calculations produce divergent integrals — infinite loop corrections to particle masses, charges, and scattering amplitudes. Renormalisation resolves this: the "bare" parameters of the Lagrangian are unphysical infinities that cancel against the infinite loop corrections, leaving finite, physically measurable "renormalised" parameters. The procedure was developed by Tomonaga, Schwinger, Feynman, and Dyson (Nobel Prize 1965) for QED, and extended to the full Standard Model by 't Hooft and Veltman (Nobel Prize 1999). Renormalisation is not mathematical sleight-of-hand — the renormalisation group (Wilson, Nobel 1982) provides a deep physical understanding: QFT describes physics at a given scale, with short-distance physics absorbed into renormalised couplings that "run" (change value) with the energy scale probed.
