The Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen Paradox (1935)

Albert Einstein, Boris Podolsky, and Nathan Rosen published a landmark paper in 1935 arguing that quantum mechanics is incomplete. Their thought experiment: two particles interact and then separate. Measuring the position of particle A instantly determines the position of particle B (by conservation of momentum). But in quantum mechanics, before measurement, neither particle has a definite position. This "spooky action at a distance" — faster-than-light influence — seemed to Einstein to prove that quantum mechanics must be missing "elements of physical reality" — hidden variables that predetermine all outcomes. For decades this remained a philosophical debate. In 1964, John Bell proved it was experimentally testable.

The Entangled State

The spin-singlet state of two spin-½ particles is the canonical entangled state:

Singlet State — Maximally Entangled|ψ⁻⟩ = (1/√2)(|↑_A↓_B⟩ − |↓_A↑_B⟩)

This state cannot be written as a product |ψ_A⟩⊗|ψ_B⟩ — the two particles are inseparable, regardless of spatial separation. If Alice measures spin-up along z, Bob's particle is instantly in spin-down along z, anywhere in the universe. The correlations hold for measurement along any axis: E(a,b) = −cos(θ_ab) where θ_ab is the angle between Alice's and Bob's measurement directions.

Bell's Theorem and the CHSH Inequality

John Bell (1964) proved that any local hidden variable (LHV) theory — one where measurement outcomes are predetermined by local variables carried by each particle — must satisfy a mathematical inequality. The experimentally accessible form (Clauser, Horne, Shimony, Holt, 1969):

CHSH Bell Inequality (any LHV theory)S = |E(a,b) − E(a,b') + E(a',b) + E(a',b')| ≤ 2

Quantum mechanics predicts S = 2√2 ≈ 2.828 for the optimal measurement angles (a=0°, a'=45°, b=22.5°, b'=67.5°). Every experiment finds S > 2, with the best measurements giving S ≈ 2.4–2.7 — definitively ruling out local realism.

Quantum Teleportation

Bennett et al. (1993) proved that entanglement enables quantum teleportation: faithful transmission of an unknown quantum state |ψ⟩ from Alice to Bob using a pre-shared entangled pair plus two classical bits. The protocol: Alice performs a Bell measurement on |ψ⟩ and her half of the entangled pair, sends the 2-bit result to Bob, who applies one of four unitary corrections to recover |ψ⟩ exactly. The original state at Alice is destroyed (no-cloning theorem). Demonstrated at distances >1,400 km via China's Micius satellite (2020).

🏆 Nobel Prize in Physics 2022

Alain Aspect, John Clauser, and Anton Zeilinger shared the 2022 Nobel Prize in Physics "for experiments with entangled photons, establishing the violation of Bell inequalities and pioneering quantum information science." The Nobel Committee stated the work "settled an almost 90-year debate about the foundations of quantum mechanics, definitively proving that quantum mechanics is non-local." Their experiments launched the entire field of quantum information technology, which now commands global investments exceeding $35 billion annually.
