The Operator Formalism — Postulates of Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics rests on a set of postulates that connect the mathematical formalism to experiment. The operator postulate is central: every physically measurable quantity (observable) is represented by a Hermitian (self-adjoint) linear operator on the system's Hilbert space. Hermitian operators have real eigenvalues — essential since experimental measurements yield real numbers.

Key Quantum Mechanical Operators Position: x̂ = x (multiply by x)    [m]

The Heisenberg Picture

There are two equivalent pictures of quantum dynamics. In the Schrödinger picture (used so far), states evolve in time and operators are constant. In the Heisenberg picture, states are constant and operators evolve:
