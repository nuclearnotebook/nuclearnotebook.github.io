The Problem with Accelerating Frames

Newton's Laws (as formulated) hold exactly only in inertial frames — frames that are neither accelerating nor rotating. But we live on Earth's surface, which is both accelerating (orbital motion around Sun) and rotating (once per day). For most everyday calculations, these effects are negligible. But for long-range ballistics, meteorology, oceanography, aerospace navigation, and precision experiments, the rotation of Earth introduces significant fictitious forces that must be accounted for.

Fictitious Forces in Linearly Accelerating Frames

An observer in a frame accelerating with acceleration a⃗₀ relative to an inertial frame must add a fictitious force −ma⃗₀ to each object to apply Newton's Second Law within their frame:

Fictitious Force (Accelerating Frame)F⃗_fictitious = −m·a⃗₀     (applied to every object of mass m)

Examples: In an upward-accelerating elevator (a₀ = +2 m/s² upward), a 70 kg person feels an effective weight of m(g+a₀) = 70×11.81 = 827 N — heavier than normal. In free fall (a₀ = g), the fictitious force exactly cancels gravity — weightlessness. This is Einstein's equivalence principle: a uniform gravitational field is indistinguishable from a uniformly accelerating frame.

Rotating Frames — Two Fictitious Forces

For an observer in a frame rotating with angular velocity Ω⃗ relative to an inertial frame, the equation of motion for a particle with velocity v⃗' (as measured in the rotating frame) is:

Equation of Motion in Rotating Framem(d²r⃗/dt²)_rot = F⃗_real + F⃗_Coriolis + F⃗_centrifugal
Coriolis ForceF⃗_Cor = −2m(Ω⃗ × v⃗')     (depends on velocity in rotating frame)
Centrifugal ForceF⃗_cf = −mΩ⃗ × (Ω⃗ × r⃗) = mΩ²r⃗_⊥     (outward from rotation axis)

The Coriolis Effect on Earth

Earth's angular velocity: Ω = 7.272 × 10⁻⁵ rad/s. At latitude φ, the effective Coriolis acceleration on a horizontally moving object at speed v is:

Horizontal Coriolis Accelerationa_Cor = 2Ωv sinφ     (rightward in Northern Hemisphere, leftward in Southern)

Consequences of Coriolis deflection on Earth:

  • Cyclones and anticyclones: Low-pressure systems spin anticlockwise (N) / clockwise (S). High-pressure systems reverse.
  • Trade winds and jet streams: Global air circulation patterns deflected by Coriolis to create the prevailing wind belts.
  • Ocean currents: Major ocean gyres deflected by Coriolis — clockwise in North Atlantic, counterclockwise in South Atlantic.
  • Long-range ballistics: A shell fired 10 km due north at 800 m/s is deflected ~100 m eastward by Coriolis at 45°N.
  • Geostrophic balance: In the atmosphere and ocean, large-scale flow is dominated by the balance between pressure gradient force and Coriolis force.

The Centrifugal Effect on Earth

The centrifugal force (outward from Earth's rotation axis) reduces effective gravity and causes Earth's oblate shape. At the equator (r⊥ = R_E = 6.371 × 10⁶ m): a_cf = Ω²R_E = (7.27×10⁻⁵)² × 6.37×10⁶ = 0.034 m/s². This is 0.35% of g — enough to make Earth's equatorial radius 21 km larger than its polar radius.

The Foucault Pendulum — Demonstrating Earth's Rotation

Foucault's pendulum (1851) elegantly demonstrates Earth's rotation without looking at the sky. The pendulum swings in a fixed plane in space. Earth rotates beneath it, so the swing plane appears to precess relative to the Earth's surface. The precession rate:

Foucault Pendulum Precession RateΩ_precess = Ω_Earth sinλ     [λ = latitude]

At the poles (λ = 90°): one full rotation in 24 hours. At the equator (λ = 0°): zero precession. At Paris (λ = 48.9°): period = 24/sin(48.9°) = 31.8 hours. The precession is clockwise in the Northern Hemisphere and counterclockwise in the Southern.

The Equivalence Principle — Gateway to General Relativity

The observation that a uniform gravitational field is locally indistinguishable from a uniformly accelerating frame (both produce the same fictitious force on test masses) became Einstein's equivalence principle — the foundation stone of General Relativity. In GR, gravity is not a force but the geometry of curved spacetime. "Free fall" is the natural inertial motion in curved spacetime, and standing on Earth's surface is the accelerating frame (accelerating outward, away from Earth's centre, in the curved spacetime sense). The fictitious forces of non-inertial frames thus contain the seed of Einstein's deepest theory of gravity.

🌍 GPS and Rotating Frames

GPS satellites orbit in near-inertial frames. Ground receivers on Earth's surface are in a rotating frame. The Sagnac effect — a phase difference between light signals sent in opposite directions around a rotating path — must be corrected for in GPS calculations. Without this correction (≈207 ns/day accumulated Sagnac phase), GPS would accumulate position errors of ~60 m per day. Both special relativistic (time dilation from satellite velocity) and general relativistic (gravitational time dilation from altitude) corrections must also be applied — making GPS the most relativistically sensitive system in everyday use.
